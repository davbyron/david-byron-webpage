ABOUT
Nice to meet you on the web! I'm David, a Computational Linguist at Lymba.

At Lymba, I work primarily with NLP tools and knowledge graphs. My day-to-day can really vary depending on the client, but I'm often responsible for ontology building, creating NLP resources, UI/UX design, various web development tasks, maintaining RESTful Python APIs, client demos, documentation writing, and quality assurance testing.

Recently, I've found myself trying to mesh my more recent aptitude for computer science with my lifelong love for art. As a result, I've engaged more and more in web development and UI/UX design in recent years. I love the challenge of making a webpage both beautiful and accessible; and constructing a product end-to-end has proven a rewarding experience. I'm looking to do more of that in the future, hopefully bringing new creative UI/UX concepts to life.

Throughout my education, I focused primarily on syntactic phenomena in hopes of finding the answers to the following questions: (i) what are the fundamental syntactic operations provided by the human language faculty?; and (ii) what constraints are they subject to, and why? This work culminated in my Master's thesis "Extraction from Conjuncts in Khoekhoe: An Argument for Cyclic Linearization" advised by Erik Zyman at the University of Chicago, a PDF of which is available below.

Outside of work, I'm a huge cooking fanatic. Having worked my way through the restaurant industry up to a front of house position in a Michelin-starred brewpub (now closed), I've come to learn a lot of new and thoughtful ways to prepare food and drinks. Other than this, I spend a lot of time cycling, gardening, playing video games, making art of all kinds, and at drag events.

PROJECTS
computational linguistics
A First Shot at Speech Recognition for Khoekhoe - Early steps toward a fully functioning speech recognition system for Khoekhoe. Predicts phones in the language using convolutional neural networks via fastai and PyTorch with an average accuracy of 30-35%. (link)

A Comparison of TF-IDF, Word2Vec, and Transfer Learning for Text Classification - A step-by-step notebook that utilizes regular expressions, n-grams, SpaCy, gensim, and neural networks with disaster tweet data in order to compare the predictive accuracy of some of the most widely used text classification approaches today. (link)

Hidden Markov Model - A statistical Markov model built in Python from scratch that predicts consonants and vowels in any Latin alphabet-using language using the Baum-Welch algorithm. (repository)

Academics
Extraction from Conjuncts in Khoekhoe: An Argument for Cyclic Linearization - In this thesis, I tackle a violation of the otherwise highly robust Coordinate Structure Constraint, so-called SLF-coordination, that has yet to be fully understood. I argue that the cyclic linearization approach to the locality of movement makes it possible to account for this puzzling phenomenon as it manifests in the Khoisan language Khoekhoe. (link)

Grammatical Sketch of |Xam - A webpage outlining the syntactic properties of |Xam, a now extinct Khoisan language. (link)
